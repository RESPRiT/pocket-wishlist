References

[edit | edit source]
  • The description is a reference to the song "It Don't Mean a Thing (If It Ain't Got That Swing)" by Duke Ellington.
  • The description may also be a reference to the jazz standard "Jitterbug Waltz" by Fats Waller.
  • The familiar equipment is a reference to Jitterbug Perfume by Tom Robbins.
  • The normal combat message mentioning partying like it's 1925 or 1998 refers not only to the song "1999" by Prince, but also to the fact that swing dancing (to which the Jitterbug is closely related) was a common dance style both in 1925 near its birth, and again in 1998 during a short-lived swing dance revival.
