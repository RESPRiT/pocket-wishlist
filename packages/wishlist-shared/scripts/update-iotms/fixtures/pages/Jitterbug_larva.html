jitterbug larva

This is the larval form of that rascal, the jitterbug. Jitterbugs are saucy, insouciant insects. They're known for their dancing, flirting, and making people describe them with obscure adjectives like "insouciant." The larva seems to be jumping and jiving, but you don't hear any wailing from it. Yet.

Notes

[edit | edit source]
  • Formerly Available in Mr. Store for 2 Mr. Accessories.
  • Introduced on October 6, 2006 as a replacement for the calm attention-deficit demon.
  • First prize in The Raffle House on January 23, 2008.

References

[edit | edit source]
  • The second-to-last sentence of the description is a reference to the song Jump, Jive, and Wail by Louis Prima (and later covered by Brian Setzer among others).
  • The "When Used" message is a reference to The Jitterbug, a song from the soundtrack of The Wizard of Oz, sung by Judy Garland.
