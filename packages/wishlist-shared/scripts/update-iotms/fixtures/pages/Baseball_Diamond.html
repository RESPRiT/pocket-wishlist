Notes

[edit | edit source]
  • Is automatically placed in your inventory for you at the beginning of your run, with the message:
    You throw up your open hand just in time to catch your Baseball Diamond as it arcs out of the afterlife behind you. Ouch, you need a glove!
    You acquire an item: Baseball Diamond
  • Has a [play ball] link.
    • Takes you to Play Ball!.
    • Clicking the link before recruiting 9 monsters gives:

      You need to recruit X foe(s) to play baseball.

      where X is the number of remaining monsters.
    • Clicking the link once you have already played 3 innings that day gives:

      You've already pitched 3 whole innings today. You better stop before you blow out your shoulder.

  • Automatically tries to recruit monsters at the end of battle:
You recruit a <monster> to play baseball. You need X more players.
  • or, once you have enough players:
You recruit a <monster> to play baseball. You are ready to play ball!
  • or, if the monster image is not exactly 100x100 pixels:
This foe is too big for baseball.
  • or, if the monster's name was modified:
This foe is too strange for baseball.
  • or, if the monster was a boss or an uncopyable monster:
This foe is too cool for baseball.
  • Reasons for why a monster would be considered too strange for baseball include: generated monster names (e.g., Copperhead Club bartender, Slime Tube monster), Monster Modifiers (some of the modifiers causing the monster to become too big instead) and Eldritch Intellect for normally valid Eldritch Tentacles. Despite Hobopolis hobos having generated names, they are considered valid baseball players. Monsters that have had their hearts stolen are also valid.
  • Can be placed in The Eternity Codpiece, which continues to allow access to the skills when worn.
